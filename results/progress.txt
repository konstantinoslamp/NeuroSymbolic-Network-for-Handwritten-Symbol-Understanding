================================================================
  PHASE A: NEUROLOG 3-Way Ablation
  Source file: results/neurolog_torch_ablation.json
  Reference:   Tsamoura & Michael, AAAI 2021
================================================================

Setup:
  - Task:         Arithmetic expression recognition (MNIST digits + operators)
  - Train size:   300 samples
  - Val / Test:   100 / 100 samples
  - Epochs:       8
  - Architecture: PyTorch CNN (T4 GPU, ~1 min total)
  - 3 conditions: Pure Neural | NGA (Neural-Guided Abduction) | WMC (Semantic Loss)

------------------------------------------------------------------
  KEY FINDINGS — PHASE A
------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. WMC achieves the best calibration (ECE 0.065 vs 0.096 for Pure
     Neural) — a 32% reduction in calibration error. This confirms
     that semantic loss integrates symbolic constraints into the
     neural training signal, producing better-calibrated predictions.

  2. Pure Neural achieves the highest task accuracy (74.3% result
     accuracy) because it optimises directly on labels without the
     overhead of abductive proof search.

  3. NGA underperforms both other methods (48.4% result accuracy,
     ECE 0.155). The abductive targets used as supervision are noisy
     when the symbolic module has limited coverage, causing the
     neural module to learn an inconsistent signal.

  4. WMC loss starts high (5.28 at epoch 1) and decreases steadily,
     reflecting the negative log of the weighted model count.

  Runtime: Pure Neural ~0.78s | NGA ~6.27s | WMC ~0.61s (GPU)


================================================================
  PHASE B: Evolvable Policies — Evolutionary Rule Learning
  Source file: results/evolvable_torch_results (2).json
  Reference:   Thoma, Vassiliades & Michael, 2026
================================================================

Setup:
  - Atoms:          4 (a1, a2, a3, a4)
  - Target rules:   4
  - Policy seed:    42
  - Train / Val / Test: 300 / 100 / 100 samples
  - Train epochs per offspring: 8
  - Max generations: 15  (early stop at 95% val accuracy)
  - Offspring per generation: ~22+
      S0  x 2  (clone + neural mutation)
      S+  x 20 (10 rule-addition variants x 2 neural mutations)
      S-  x 2k (k = literals in latest rule body, x 2 neural mutations)
  - Architecture: PyTorch AtomCNN (T4 GPU)

Target policy (hidden from learner):
  R1 :: a2, a1 implies -h
  R2 :: a1, a4, a3, -a2 implies h
  R3 :: -a4, a3 implies -h
  R4 :: -a4, a2 implies h

------------------------------------------------------------------
  KEY FINDINGS — PHASE B
------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. The organism started with an empty policy (100% abstain at
     generation 0) and discovered a working symbolic policy purely
     through evolutionary search — no gradient on the policy rules.

  2. S+ (rule addition) mutations drove all major accuracy gains:
     gens 1, 2, 4, 10, 11, 12 were all S+ selections. This confirms
     the elaboration-tolerance property of MC semantics — new rules
     appended at the end override conflicting earlier rules.

  3. Gens 7-9 show a detrimental exploration phase (accuracy dipped
     from 75% to 70%). The algorithm continued rather than freezing
     (per paper spec: select best detrimental, never keep parent).
     This exploration enabled the breakthrough at gen 10-12.

  4. Final test accuracy 99% — near-perfect recovery of the hidden
     symbolic policy from noisy MNIST-based atom observations.

  5. Early stop at generation 12 (val 97% >= threshold 95%),
     demonstrating efficient convergence.

  Runtime: 432s / 7.2 min on T4 GPU
           (previous broken run: 90s flat at 57% — old code)
